# Paper Communication Suite Messaging Design

## Goal

Make the research homepage communicate a practical downstream value of AutoDesign: one paper can become the complete set of artifacts needed for research dissemination and conference participation.

The messaging must remain aligned with the paper's evidence boundary. Academic posters are the validated PosterHarness instantiation. Slides, research webpages, and narrated videos are exploratory generalization studies produced from the broader meta-harness optimization framework.

## Core Message

Use a two-level claim:

1. AutoDesign studies the system that produces research communication artifacts.
2. From one paper, that system can produce a poster, slides, a research webpage, and a narrated video with synchronized subtitles.

The homepage must not imply that all four formats have equal maturity or equal empirical validation.

## Placement

### Hero

Add one concise supporting sentence beneath the current research framing:

> From one paper to a complete research communication suite: an editable poster, presentation slides, a research webpage, and a narrated conference video with synchronized subtitles.

This sentence introduces the output range without turning the research homepage into a product landing page.

### Artifact Studies

Rename the section headline to:

> One paper.  
> A complete communication suite.

Replace the current supporting paragraph with:

> AutoDesign's validated PosterHarness produces source-grounded academic posters through autonomous optimization and human-guided refinement. We further study how the same meta-harness optimization framework generalizes to slides, research webpages, and narrated videos.

Add a compact process line above the tabs:

> Paper -> Poster · Slides · Web · Narrated Video

The process line is a visual summary, not an additional navigation control.

## Artifact Status Language

Keep the four existing tabs and viewers. Refine the metadata so each format communicates both its use case and evidence status.

| Artifact | Use case | Evidence status |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Poster | Conference presentation | Validated PosterHarness output; autonomous optimization and human-guided refinement |
| Slides | Conference talk | Exploratory meta-harness generalization study |
| Web | Research dissemination | Exploratory meta-harness generalization study |
| Video | Narrated conference presentation with synchronized subtitles | Exploratory meta-harness generalization study |

The Video panel must explicitly mention both generated narration and synchronized subtitles in visible body copy or metadata.

## Visual Treatment

- Preserve the current research-report visual system: serif display typography, restrained green accents, ruled metadata, and square geometry.
- Keep the new suite message within the existing Hero and Artifact Studies composition.
- Do not add a separate marketing section, feature cards, decorative icons, or a product-style pricing/benefits layout.
- The process line should read as a compact research workflow label and must not compete with the section title.
- Retain the existing interactive artifact tabs and viewers.

## Claim Boundaries

- Do not describe Slides, Web, or Video as validated production systems.
- Do not imply that PosterHarness directly produces every format.
- Attribute cross-format exploration to the meta-harness optimization framework.
- Describe Poster as the validated and human-refined instantiation.
- Describe narration and subtitles as demonstrated Video capabilities, not as independently benchmarked results.

## Verification

- The Hero states the complete output suite within the first viewport without displacing the research framing.
- The Artifact Studies heading communicates the one-paper-to-many-artifacts value before users open a tab.
- Poster remains visibly distinguished from the three exploratory formats.
- Video visibly states narration and synchronized subtitles.
- Existing Poster, Slides, Web, and Video interactions continue to pass browser tests.
- Desktop and mobile screenshots show no text clipping, crowding, or overlap.
- Production-domain QA confirms the revised copy and all four artifact viewers.
